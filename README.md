# Job Preferences: A Conjoint Analysis 
This project uses Choice-Based Conjoint (CBC) methodology to quantify how people value different job attributes. By forcing respondents to rank job offers with varying characteristics, we move beyond simple "wish lists" to discover what people are actually willing to sacrifice (eg, how much salary is worth a remote work setup?).
# The Attributes & Levels
here people analyzed 9 unique job profiles based on four key attributes:

Base Salary: €25k, €30k, €33k

Work Location: In Office, Hybrid, Remote

Annual Bonus: 0% Fixed, 10% of Base, 25% of Performance

Company Size: Small, Mid-size, Big Company

# Data collection

The Conjoint Analysis reveals that People prioritize Company Size (28.82%) and Work Location (27.72%) as the dominant factors in their career decisions, collectively outweighing the importance of base salary. This demographic exhibits a clear preference for a "balanced" professional life, favoring mid-sized companies and hybrid work arrangements while actively rejecting the isolation of fully remote roles and the high-pressure environment associated with large corporations. Notably, the study identifies a "Salary Sacrifice" threshold where the utility of a 30 000€ salary paired with a 25% performance bonus exceeds that of the highest salary tier, suggesting that these students are meritocratic and willing to trade a higher base paycheck for a superior work-life balance and the opportunity to be rewarded for individual impact.
